Overview

Hollywood is an entertainment icon, and famous landmarks such as the TCL Chinese Theater and the star-studded Walk of Fame attract a huge number of tourists. Other highlights include Paramount Pictures and established music venues, such as the Hollywood Bowl and the Dolby Theater where the Academy Awards are held. Stand-up comedy clubs, vintage and unique bars and fancy nightclubs cater to visitors of all kinds. The locals mean the restaurants scattered in the Thai Quarter.

History

Hollywood was laid out as a subdivision in 1887 by Harvey Wilcox, who was a prohibitionist from Kansas. However, real-estate magnate H.J. Whitley transformed Hollywood into a wealthy and popular residential area. Hollywood became a municipality in 1903 and was incorporated into Los Angeles in 1910.
