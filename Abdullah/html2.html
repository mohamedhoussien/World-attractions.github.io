Cambridge castle

It is a castle located in Cambridge, in the county of Cambridgeshire, England The castle was originally built after the Norman conquest of England in order to control the important strategic route to the north and the castle played an important role in the struggles of the anarchist war and the wars of the First and Second Barons. After its massive expansion by Edward I, the castle fell into disrepair at the end of the Middle Ages, and its stones were used to build the surrounding colleges During the English Civil War, the castle was reused and fortified again, but it was soon neglected again; It was used as a county prison The castle was demolished after the construction of a new prison for the county in 1842 AD in its courtyard. The prison was also demolished in 1932




Durham Castle

Durham Castle is a Norman castle in the city of Durham, England which since 1840 has all been owned by Durham University The university is open to the general public for visiting but only with the accompaniment of a tour guide, because it is used as a business building and accommodation for more than 100 university students. [1][2][3] The castle is located on top of a hill above the River Wear on the Durham Peninsula opposite Durham Cathedral




Highclere Castle

Highclere Castle caught the eye during its appearance in the popular TV series Downton Abbey and it is located in Hampshire where the four parts of this famous series were filmed. Initially a medieval palace, this castle was transformed in the mid-19th century by Sir Charles Barry , the architect who invented the Houses of Parliament in London. Movie Downton Abbey, you can explore gardens dating back to the 13th century along with 1,000 acres stunning gardens designed by renowned landscaper Capability Brown Today, the Earl and Countess of Carnarvon live in the castle, after their family had lived there since 1679.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the British monarchs. It is located in London,and is considered a place for most of the events related to the British royal family, and a place for many meetings and visits of heads of state, in addition to being a major tourist attraction
